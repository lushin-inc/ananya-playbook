Rule 1

You're the salesperson for the next six months

You can't manage a salesperson if you've never done what they do — you won't know if they're doing it well, and you won't be able to call them out when they're not. The self-serve pivot may reduce the sales motion later, but for the next six months, the account executive is you. Then hire.

Rule 2

Work the three things in order

All of sales comes down to three things. The order matters — most people skip to #3 and wonder why nothing works.

1

Quality of prospect

  • Right companies → right people at those companies → clean list
    • Yours: ecom/Shopify brands running 40+ Meta ads
    • Reach the founder or head of marketing
    • Already done — 177 contacts across 133 companies, verified emails (download below)
2

Quality of message

  • Speak to specific pains they have, as it relates to what you sell
  • Every prospect is making one of two decisions: do something vs. nothing, or pick Hypesonic vs. the other option
  • Yours is the second — speak to the pain of their current way (human creators, other tools, doing it themselves)
3

Quantity

  • Once 1 and 2 are right, it's just a numbers game
  • That's the Cookbook (Rule 4)
  • Like the cookie stencil — get it right once, then stamp out a bunch
Rule 3

Start your message with their pain

Commercials start with the problem (the messy kitchen) before they ever show the product, because pain is what sticks in people's brains. Your outreach and your answer to "so what do you do?" should start the same way:

Talk track — when they ask what you do

"I run a company called Hypesonic. A lot of our customers, before they worked with us, said things like...

"After a few weeks our Ads stop working and we're right back where we started"

or....

"We spend a ton on creative half the time we're just guessing."

or lastly...

"By the time we get new ads made, tested, and approved..... it's too late...."

Based on those.... any reason to talk.... further?......"

Rule 4

Cookbook: manage behavior, not results

If you manage results, you're managing the past. If you manage behavior, you're managing the future. Pick activity types you'll actually do, commit to a weekly number, and hit it.

The documentary-crew test: only things a camera crew following you around could see you do count. Actions, not thoughts.

Rule 5

Bias toward action

Paul Lushin's line: "If you're asking how, you haven't bought the decision yet." Dan's first move out of the '09 recession was handing out 200 flyers for odd jobs — hyper-inefficient, but that one backyard job led to the restaurant job that paid off his debt in six months. Imperfect action produces the clue to the next action. Perfecting the recipe produces nothing.

Reagan and Sarah say you're insanely smart. That's the risk — smart people see every connection and over-complicate. Take the dumb action. Get it done.

Don't do this

Don't spend the week perfecting. No perfect copy, no perfect list, no waiting on final pricing. Send with what you have, then bring Dan the results — that's what you'll work on together next.
